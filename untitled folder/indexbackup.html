The Challenge


WebVR is a very under-utilized technology with promising possibilities. This thesis was an exploration into how I could implement WebVR into a current business, to see if I could give them a competitive edge over their rivals.

The business I chose to focus on and work with was a paragliding company in Ontario.

Key Findings


.

Finding - If a person is interested in an activity, experiencing a 360 video of it significantly increases their longing to do that activity.

Finding - If a person is not interesteed in an activity, experiencing a 360 video of it significantly increases their distaste for doing that activity.

.

Implication: The immersiveness of 360 videos do a very good job filtering out the target audience for me.



Measuring Success


.

Desireability Study
Step 1
Taking users through a journey of finding the current website. Finding out do they have any interest in the activity at all, after seeing the content on the site.

Step 2
Allowing users to engage with the 360 video of that activity. Then taking them through the same journey to find the same website. To see if and how their level of interest changed after seeing the content on the site.

Guarantee
The implication of this meant that I could garantee with a fair bit of confidence that 360 videos seem to make it more likely for those interseted in doing this, to make the jump from thinking about it to doing it.

By also implementing this now, the company is strategically placing intself, in a place where it can take advantage of what webvr will become in the next 10 years. This plan may not show immediate results but is aimed at showing long lasting effects.
